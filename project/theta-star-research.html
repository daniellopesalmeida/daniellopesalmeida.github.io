Theta* Research
Overview
This project focuses on researching and implementing Theta* pathfinding, exploring how an any-angle approach can produce smoother paths than classic grid-based search. It also highlights how Theta* can create more efficient routes in open areas when compared to A*.
It was developed for the Gameplay Programming Research Project course in C++, where we were encouraged to go beyond the material covered in class and study a new topic independently. I chose to focus on pathfinding algorithms, with Theta* standing out as an interesting extension of A* because of its line-of-sight optimization and more natural path results.
This project combines algorithmic thinking with practical implementation and visual validation. It gave me the opportunity to work on navigation, spatial reasoning, and AI movement behavior, while also better understanding how pathfinding choices affect the feel and efficiency of agent movement in a game environment.
